Your Goal-Setting Mission

A business without goals is just a hobby. Amateurs wait for motivation; professionals create it through clear, written goals. Here is your mission to complete this week.

  1. Set Activity Goals, Not Just Result Goals. You can't control if someone joins, but you CAN control your activity. Start with a simple goal: "I will talk to 2 new people about this business/product every day."
  2. Schedule Your Work. Block out 30-60 minutes in your calendar each day for "Income Producing Activities" (IPAs). This is your time to contact people, follow up, and learn. Protect this time fiercely.
  3. Leverage Your Upline. Your job isn't to be the expert—it's to be the bridge. Invite people to a 3-way call or Zoom with your upline (Edi Jusic, Marco Berg, Eskil Kaspersen, etc.). This is how you learn and earn simultaneously. Your goal: Book one 3-way call this week.
  4. Watch and Learn. Go to the YouTube channels for Frazer Brookes and Daniel G to understand what professionals do every day.

How to Invite to Calls (The Right Way)

Your goal is to "peak interest," not to be the expert. Keep it simple and let the tool (the call/webinar) do the work. Try this:

"Hey [Name], are you open to checking out some information on a project I'm working on to create another stream of income? It may or may not be for you, but I thought of you and wanted to show you. My partners are running a live call tonight that explains it perfectly."

Build Your Initial Contact List

Don't prejudge! Your goal is not to sell everyone, but to inform everyone. Take out your notebook and do a "Memory Jogger." Write down names from your phone contacts, social media friends, old colleagues, family, etc. Aim for 50-100 names. This is your starting inventory.
